The real estate sales challenge

Real estate loses more leads to process failures
than to competitors.

A real estate buyer submits enquiries on three portals in ten minutes. Each portal sends the lead to a different place. One goes to a spreadsheet, one to a WhatsApp group, one to an email inbox no one monitors. By the time an agent calls back — if they call back — the buyer has already spoken to someone else.

This is not a sales problem. It is a process problem. The product, the pricing and the location may all be right — but the lead was lost in the gap between where it arrived and where the agent could act on it. This is the gap telecrm closes for every real estate business that runs on it.

What telecrm solves for real estate
telecrm gives real estate teams a single pipeline for every lead from every source — deduplicated, assigned instantly, tracked through every stage from first call to site visit to booking — so no lead goes cold because of a process failure.
What real estate teams need from a CRM

Five things that determine whether
a portal lead becomes a booking.

Real estate CRM requirements are specific. A generic CRM handles contacts and tasks. A real estate CRM needs to handle the full journey from portal lead to possession — with the property, project and agent context that makes that journey trackable.

1
Instant capture from every portal — no manual entry
Housing.com, 99acres, MagicBricks, Square Yards, Facebook Lead Ads and direct website enquiries all need to flow into one system automatically the moment a buyer submits. Any lead that requires manual entry before it reaches an agent is a lead that will be delayed or dropped. Real estate moves too fast for manual processes at the top of the funnel.
2
Deduplication across sources
A serious buyer submits on multiple portals. Without deduplication, the same buyer is assigned to three different agents who call within minutes of each other — creating a terrible first impression and wasting agent time. A real estate CRM needs to identify the same buyer across sources and consolidate into one lead record before assignment.
3
Site visit scheduling and GPS-verified tracking
The site visit is the most critical stage in the real estate sales process. It needs to be scheduled inside the CRM, tracked to completion, and logged with GPS verification and a photo. Without visit tracking, managers have no way to know which leads have been taken to site, which agent took them or what the outcome was — beyond asking the agent themselves.
4
Post-booking pipeline for possession management
The sale does not end at booking. Payment milestones, document collection, construction updates, possession scheduling — all of this requires structured follow-up across a timeline that can span months or years. Real estate businesses that manage post-booking through WhatsApp groups and spreadsheets lose customer confidence and miss revenue from referrals and repeat purchases.
5
Channel partner and broker pipeline visibility
Developers running channel partner programs need to track which CP sourced which lead, what stage those leads are at and how much commission has been earned — without giving every CP access to the full database. A real estate CRM needs role-based access that gives channel partners visibility into their own leads only, while the developer sees the full picture across all CPs.
How telecrm works for real estate

From portal enquiry to possession —
every stage managed in one system.

telecrm is used by real estate developers, brokers, channel partners and property management businesses across India. Every capability below is available on mobile — so agents working in the field have the same system as managers at their desk.

Portal lead capture
Every lead from Housing.com, 99acres, MagicBricks, Square Yards and Facebook flows into telecrm automatically. No manual entry, no delayed assignment — the lead is in the system and with an agent within seconds of submission.
Automatic deduplication
When the same buyer appears across multiple portals, telecrm identifies the duplicate and consolidates into a single lead record before assignment. One buyer, one agent, one conversation — not three agents calling the same number.
Site visit scheduling
Site visits are scheduled inside the lead record and tracked to completion. GPS check-in and photo capture at the site create a verified visit log — so managers know which leads have been taken to site, by which agent, and what the buyer's response was.
Project-wise pipeline stages
Configure separate pipelines per project — with the stages, custom fields and follow-up rules specific to each development. New enquiry → site visit → negotiation → booking → agreement → possession. Every stage tracked with timestamps and agent accountability.
WhatsApp follow-up automation
Automated WhatsApp messages trigger at each stage — instant acknowledgement on lead capture, brochure on first contact, site visit reminder 24 hours before, post-visit follow-up, booking congratulations. Every buyer gets a consistent, timely communication experience.
Channel partner management
Channel partners and brokers are onboarded into telecrm with access to their own leads only. Developers see sourcing attribution, stage progression and commission tracking across the full CP network — without exposing the full database to any individual partner.
1-click calling with auto-logging
Agents call buyers directly from the lead record. Every call is recorded and logged automatically — duration, recording and outcome against the lead — so managers can review calls, coach agents and track response times without asking for manual reports.
Post-booking follow-up pipeline
After booking, the customer moves into a dedicated post-sale pipeline — payment milestone tracking, document collection reminders, construction update communications and possession scheduling. The relationship continues to be managed systematically until possession is complete.
Manager dashboards
Sales managers see the full pipeline in real time — leads by source, by agent, by stage, by project. Site visit conversion rates, call volumes, follow-up compliance and booking velocity — all visible without requesting reports from the team.
Real estate business types

telecrm works for every type of
real estate business in India.

The sales process differs between a large developer, a boutique broker and a channel partner network — but the core need is the same: every lead captured, every agent accountable, every stage tracked.

Residential Developers
Large project launches
Developers launching residential projects need to handle high lead volumes from multiple portals simultaneously, manage site visit scheduling at scale and track CP-sourced leads separately from direct enquiries — all in real time during launch windows.
Real Estate Brokers
Multi-agent brokerage operations
Brokerages managing 10–100 agents across multiple projects need complete visibility into every agent's pipeline, call activity and site visit record — so management can identify underperformance and intervene before leads go cold.
Channel Partners
CP network management
Channel partners working across multiple developer projects need a CRM that shows their leads, tracks their pipeline and records their commission — without requiring a separate system for each developer they represent.
Commercial Real Estate
Long-cycle B2B property deals
Commercial real estate deals involve multiple stakeholders, long decision timelines and complex negotiation stages. telecrm tracks multi-stakeholder deals, logs every meeting and call and manages the full negotiation pipeline to closure.
Co-Working & Managed Spaces
Enquiry to occupancy pipeline
Co-working operators managing space enquiries, trial bookings and seat conversions need a pipeline that reflects the short, fast sales cycle specific to managed workspace — from initial enquiry to trial to full occupancy agreement.
Plots & Land Sales
Field sales with site visits
Plot sales involve extensive field activity — agents visiting multiple sites with prospective buyers across large geographies. GPS-verified visit tracking and mobile CRM access give managers real-time visibility into what's happening in the field.
