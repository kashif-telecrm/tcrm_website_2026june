The next message doesn't go out.

Before you request a demo

Questions we always get.

Can I build sequences for any industry, or only the ones shown?
Any industry. The sequences shown — Real Estate, BFSI, EdTech — are examples. The workflow builder lets you set any trigger, any condition and any message content. Automobile dealers use it for service reminders and insurance renewal. Travel agencies use it for trip anniversary offers. SaaS companies use it for plan upgrade nudges. If you can define the trigger and segment, you can build the sequence.
What happens if a customer converts partway through a sequence?
The sequence stops automatically. Before every message, telecrm re-checks the conditions you set. If the customer's status has changed — they converted, opted out or were marked Won — the next message doesn't send. This means customers who respond early never receive the remaining messages in the sequence.
How many messages can a drip sequence have?
There's no hard limit. You can build sequences with 2 messages or 10, with delays ranging from minutes to weeks. Most effective sequences have 3–5 messages spaced 24–72 hours apart, but the right length depends entirely on your sales cycle and customer behaviour.
Does the sequence work for customers already in the CRM, or only new ones?
Both. You can trigger sequences based on a new event (lead created, course completed) or on a scheduled date (renewal date approaching, subscription anniversary). Existing customers in the CRM are eligible for date-based triggers immediately once you set the renewal date field.
Do customers need to have WhatsApp for this to work?
The drip sequences use WhatsApp as the delivery channel, so customers need WhatsApp on the number in the CRM. The condition check includes a WhatsApp opt-out filter — customers who have opted out are automatically excluded from every sequence. telecrm also supports SMS as an alternate channel.
