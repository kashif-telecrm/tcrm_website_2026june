Features · Automations

Flexible automation for
every step of your process.

telecrm automations handle the repetitive work for your team — assigning leads the moment they arrive, sending the right WhatsApp at the right stage, scheduling follow-ups, and moving records between pipelines — unlock your team's full selling capacity.

01 — Workflow Automation & Stage Triggers

Your sales process,
running on autopilot.

telecrm's workflow automation lets you model your entire sales process as a set of rules — define what happens when a lead enters a stage, exits it, reaches a lifecycle milestone, or goes stale. Every rule fires in real time, every time, without exception.

  • Trigger on stage entry.
    Set a rule on any stage — Initial, Active substage, Won, or Lost — that fires the instant a lead is moved into it. One rule per stage or many; each is independent.
    Trigger — on stage entry
    Lead moves to Proposal Sent
    Any stage Fires instantly No delay
  • Trigger on stage exit.
    Rules can also fire when a lead leaves a stage — useful for ending a nurture sequence, revoking access, or notifying a team that a record has moved on.
    Trigger — on stage exit
    Lead exits Negotiating → moved to Won or Lost
    Stop sequences Notify manager Log outcome
  • Unlimited rules per pipeline.
    Every stage in every pipeline can have its own independent automation rules. Rules don't interfere with each other — each stage is its own trigger context.
    Active rules — Sales pipeline
    New Lead2 actions
    Proposal Sent3 actions
    Won4 actions
    Lost2 actions
  • Lifecycle-based triggers.
    Triggers don't have to be stage moves — they can fire based on lifecycle events too: a lead going stale after X days without a call, a deal sitting in a stage too long, a follow-up task expiring. Set conditions on time and inactivity, not just movement.
    Lifecycle trigger examples
    No call for 3 days→Alert manager
    Stage stale 7 days→Send re-engagement WA
    Task overdue 1 day→Reassign to manager
02 — Multi-Action Sequences

One trigger.
Many actions, in order.

A single stage move can fire a chain of actions — send a WhatsApp, create a follow-up task, notify the manager, reassign the lead, and move it to another pipeline — all from one rule, in sequence, without any manual intervention.

  • Chain unlimited actions per trigger.
    Add as many actions as your process requires to a single trigger. Each fires in the order you set them — there's no cap on how many actions one rule can contain.
    Rule — Lead moves to "Demo Scheduled"
    Send WhatsApp — Demo confirmation template
    Create follow-up task — Call after demo
    Notify manager — Demo booked alert
  • Available action types.
    Each action in a sequence can be one of several types — giving full flexibility to model any step in your sales or post-sales process.
    Action library
    Send WhatsApp
    Create task
    Notify rep / manager
    Reassign lead
    Move to pipeline
    Update stage
  • Delayed actions.
    Set a delay before any action fires — send a WhatsApp 30 minutes after a stage change, create a follow-up task 24 hours later. Delays are configured per action, not per rule.
    Action with delay
    Send WhatsApp — immediately
    Create task — delay 24h+24h
  • Custom trigger-based workflows.
    Build workflows from scratch to match your exact process — any trigger, any action combination. telecrm's rule engine is not limited to preset templates; every workflow is custom-configured for your pipeline, your stages, and your team's way of working.
    Custom workflow — policy renewal
    Policy renewal workflowCustom
    Trigger
    Renewal date field = 30 days from today
    Actions
    Send WhatsApp — Renewal reminder template
    Create task — Follow up on renewal · +3 days
    Notify manager — Renewal due alert

Sent automatically, every time.

  • Welcome messages on lead capture.

    Lead enters Demo Scheduled

    ✓✓ Sent automatically

    Every WhatsApp sent by an automation — template message, reminder, drip step — is recorded on the lead's activity timeline with timestamp and delivery status. Reps always know exactly what the lead has received.

04 — Drip Campaigns

Multi-step sequences that run themselves
over days, weeks or months.

Build WhatsApp drip campaigns that send a sequence of messages over days or weeks — triggered by a stage entry and then automatic from there. Cold leads get nurtured back to life. Warm leads stay warm. No rep needs to remember to follow up.

  • Multi-step sequences over days, weeks or months.
    Configure a sequence of messages with delays between each — Day 1, Day 3, Day 7. Each message fires automatically on schedule from the moment the trigger fires. No rep involvement after setup.
    Drip sequence — "Cold lead reactivation"
    Day 0 — Immediate
    Hi {{name}}, we noticed you were interested in telecrm. Still looking for a CRM? We'd love to help.
    Day 3
    Here's a 2-minute overview of what telecrm can do for your sales team. [Link]
    Day 7
    Want to see it live? Book a 30-min demo — no obligation. [Book now]
  • Stop the sequence on a reply or stage change.
    If a lead replies or is moved to a new stage, the drip sequence stops automatically. Leads that re-engage don't get bombarded with the rest of the sequence — the system knows to stop.
    Sequence auto-stopped
    Lead replied — drip sequence paused automatically
  • Personalised at scale with template variables.
    Every message in a drip sequence can use merge fields — name, company, stage, assigned rep — so each message feels personal even when it's sent to hundreds of leads simultaneously.
    Template with variables
    Hi {{name}}, your demo with {{rep_name}} is tomorrow at {{time}}. Looking forward to speaking!
  • Branch the sequence based on user action or response.
    Drip campaigns can fork based on what the lead does — if they reply, open a link, or move to a stage, the sequence takes a different path. One branch for engaged leads, another for silent ones. The right next message is determined by what the lead just did, not by a fixed schedule.
    Branching drip — based on lead response
    Day 0 — Send to Fresh leads with Sales Team field empty
    Hi {{name}}, still looking for a CRM? We'd love to show you telecrm.
    Lead responds?
    ✓ Yes — replied
    Route to rep · Create call task · Stop drip
    ✗ No response
    Day 3 · Send follow-up · Continue sequence
05 — Schedules

Bulk sends. Perfectly timed.
Always targeted.

Schedules are telecrm's timestamp-based automation — different from workflows, which fire on events or actions. A schedule fires at a specific date and time, against a filtered set of leads, and is designed for bulk operations like mass WhatsApp sends, reminders, and periodic updates. Simple, powerful, and no decision logic required.

  • Schedules vs workflows — the key difference.
    A workflow fires when something happens — a lead moves to a stage, a form is submitted, a task expires. A schedule fires at a specific timestamp, regardless of what the lead is doing. Workflows are event-driven; schedules are time-driven. Both are necessary; neither replaces the other.
    Workflow vs Schedule — at a glance
    Workflow
    Fires on an event or action — stage move, form submit, task expiry
    ✓ Decision branching
    ✓ Multi-action chains
    ✓ Conditional logic
    Schedule
    Fires at a specific timestamp — date, time, recurring
    ✓ Filter-based targeting
    ✓ Bulk operations
    ✗ No decision branching
  • Requires a filter — always targeted, never blind.
    Every schedule must define a filter before it can run — stage, source, label, city, date field, or any custom field. The schedule fires only against leads that match the filter at execution time. A schedule cannot run without a filter, and will not run if the filtered lead count exceeds 10,000.
    Schedule filter configuration
    Filter conditions
    Stage = Active AND Source = Facebook
    Matching leads 847
    Ready to schedule · Under 10,000 lead limit
  • Built for bulk operations.
    Schedules are the right tool for bulk WhatsApp sends, festive wishes, batch reminders, and periodic updates to a segment of your leads. Set the filter, set the timestamp, set the message — it fires once to all matching leads at that moment.
    Bulk WhatsApp schedule
    Diwali wishes — all active leads 847 leads
    Fires at: 20 Oct · 9:00 AM
    WA template: Festive wishes
    Will not run if filtered leads exceed 10,000
  • Timely updates and wishes to leads.
    Use schedules to send birthday wishes, anniversary congratulations, festive greetings, or periodic check-ins to a filtered segment. Because schedules are timestamp-driven and filter-targeted, they feel personal even at scale — the right message, to the right leads, at the right moment.
    Schedule use cases
    Birthday wishes Anniversary wishes Festive greetings Product updates Periodic check-ins Event invitations

Every lead assigned
the moment it arrives.

telecrm auto-assigns leads the instant they're captured — by source, by geography, by product interest, or in a round-robin rotation. No manager has to touch a lead to send it to the right rep. No lead sits unassigned while the team is busy.

  • Round-robin assignment.

FAQ

Your questions, answered plainly.

What can telecrm automations do?
Stage-based triggers, multi-action sequences, WhatsApp automation, lead-assignment rules, drip campaigns, and cross-pipeline handoffs — telecrm runs your sales process so your team doesn't have to.
How do automation triggers work?
One trigger can fire many actions in order, so the right thing happens automatically every time a lead reaches a given stage.
Can an automation run over a long period?
Yes — multi-step sequences run themselves over days, weeks, or months without anyone having to remember the next step.
Do I need technical skills to set them up?
No. Automations are built around your stages and process, so you configure them visually rather than writing code.
