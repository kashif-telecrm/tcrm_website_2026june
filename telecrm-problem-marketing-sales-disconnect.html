Problems Hub · Marketing & Sales

Marketing and sales don't share
a single view of the lead.

Marketing runs broadcasts and ads. Sales calls leads cold without seeing what was sent. Buyers hear contradicting offers and lose trust. The disconnect costs deals — and nobody can prove which campaign caused which close.

Where it breaks down

Six problems your team
is probably living with right now.

Problem 01
Marketing messages lack sales context
WhatsApp broadcasts and email campaigns go out to every lead — closed, cold, hot or won — with the same offer. A repeat customer gets a 'first-time buyer' promo. A just-closed lead gets a discount offer for a deal already signed.
Problem 02
Sales calls leads completely cold
A lead opens five emails, clicks two ads and joins a webinar — and the rep calling them has no idea. The opener sounds like a first contact when the buyer is already three-quarters of the way to a decision.
Problem 03
Same lead pitched twice, contradicting
Marketing sends a 20%-off festive offer over WhatsApp. The rep calls and quotes full price an hour later. The buyer hears two prices from the same brand in an hour. Trust breaks before the conversation begins.
Problem 04
Lead intent never reaches sales
Marketing knows who's high-intent — open rate, click-through, page visits, replies. But the rep's queue treats every lead equal. A serial clicker and a cold form-fill get the same call at the same priority.
Problem 05
Campaign engagement doesn't trigger sales
A lead replies to a broadcast asking about pricing. The reply lands in the marketing inbox, sits for two days, and gets routed to sales when the buyer has already booked elsewhere. Intent that arrived as a signal gets buried.
Problem 06
Closed-loop attribution is impossible
A deal closes, but you don't know which campaign sparked it. Marketing claims the lead, sales claims the conversion, the spreadsheet credits neither. Budget decisions for next quarter repeat the same guesswork.
How telecrm fixes it

Six fixes — one platform.

Every capability below is available on mobile and desktop, configured for your team in hours, and running the moment you go live.

One unified lead timeline
Every WhatsApp broadcast, email open, ad click, form submit and rep call lands on one lead timeline. The rep sees what marketing has said, what the buyer has engaged with and what stage the lead is at — before the first word of the opening line.
Segment-aware broadcast targeting
Segment leads by stage, source, location or any field — and broadcast only to who should receive each message. New-lead nurture goes to fresh enquiries. Renewal campaigns go to customers approaching contract end. Never the wrong message to the wrong person.
Intent-based lead prioritisation
Leads that engage with campaigns — open emails, click ads, reply to broadcasts — surface at the top of the rep's queue automatically. High-intent buyers get called first. Cold leads wait. Reps spend time where conversion probability is highest.
Campaign-reply auto-routing to sales
When a lead replies to a broadcast, telecrm routes the reply to the assigned rep immediately and creates a follow-up task. No reply sits in a marketing inbox. No intent signal gets buried. Hot inbound engagement reaches sales in seconds.
Shared lead status for marketing
Marketing sees every lead's current stage — Fresh, Warm, Hot, Closed Won, Closed Lost — in real time. Campaigns exclude closed leads automatically. Active deals aren't bombarded with acquisition offers. The two teams work from the same source of truth.
Closed-loop source attribution
From first campaign touch to closed deal, telecrm tracks the full attribution path. Which campaign sourced the lead, which sequence nurtured it, which rep closed it — all on one record. Budget decisions are made on revenue generated, not leads generated.
Also worth knowing

Built for the way
Indian sales teams actually work.

Consistent messaging across channels
Because marketing and sales share the same lead record, reps know exactly what offer a lead has seen. No contradicting prices, no duplicate pitches, no first-contact openers to buyers who have engaged twelve times already.
WhatsApp campaigns with CRM intelligence
Run WhatsApp campaigns with the lead's current stage, source and engagement history informing every message — not just their name. Personalisation that reflects where the buyer actually is in the journey, not a merge-field guess.
Automated handoff on engagement threshold
Set a campaign engagement threshold — clicked twice, opened three times, replied once — and telecrm automatically notifies the assigned rep and elevates the lead in their queue. Marketing-qualified leads reach sales at peak intent, not days later.
Single dashboard for both teams
Marketing sees source performance and campaign conversion. Sales sees rep activity and stage conversion. Leadership sees the full picture — from first ad impression to closed deal value — in one dashboard that both teams trust.
