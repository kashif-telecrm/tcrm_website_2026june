CRM by Team · Field Teams

Know exactly where your field team is
and what they are doing.

Field sales teams are the hardest to manage — they are out of sight, moving between locations all day, and their activity is traditionally self-reported. telecrm replaces verbal updates with GPS-verified check-ins, photo capture and real-time activity dashboards that give managers complete field visibility from their phone.

Who are field sales teams?

Sales teams that do their work
face to face — on the ground.

Field sales teams are any group of reps who sell by visiting customers in person — field officers visiting retailers, real estate agents taking buyers to site visits, FMCG reps covering distribution routes, agricultural equipment sales executives visiting farms, or Quick-commerce field sales teams onboarding local merchants. The sale happens on location, not over the phone.

The defining challenge for field sales teams is verification and accountability. A manager cannot see what their field reps are doing — who they visited, whether they actually went to the location, what was discussed, what the outcome was — without a system that captures it in the moment. Self-reported visit logs are unreliable. GPS-verified check-ins are not.

What telecrm solves for field teams
telecrm gives field sales managers verified, real-time visibility into every field rep's location, visit history and activity — through GPS check-ins, photo uploads and automatic call logging that capture what actually happened, not what the rep said happened.
What field teams need from a CRM

Five things that give managers genuine visibility
over a team they cannot physically observe.

Managing a field team without verified activity data means managing on trust and WhatsApp updates. Here is what a CRM for field teams needs to provide instead.

1
GPS-verified visit records — not self-reported logs
A field rep reporting "visited 8 clients today" is unverifiable without location data. A good CRM for field teams captures the GPS coordinates, timestamp and a photo at each check-in — creating a verified record of where the rep was and when, without requiring any trust in self-reporting. This is the difference between managing a field team and hoping you are managing one.
2
Territory-based lead and account assignment
Field reps should own a defined territory — a pincode cluster, a city zone or a route — and be automatically assigned leads and accounts within it. Without territory ownership, field teams have coverage gaps, overlap between reps and no clear accountability for what happens within a geography. With territory assignment, every location is owned and every rep knows exactly where they should be.
3
Visit history and revisit discipline
Field sales success often comes from consistent revisiting — showing up at the right account at the right frequency. A good field CRM tracks visit history per account, shows how many times a rep has visited and when the last visit was, and alerts when accounts within a territory have not been visited in a defined period. Revisit discipline becomes a managed behaviour, not a forgotten one.
4
Works on the mobile app — offline if needed
Field reps are rarely at a desk. A CRM for field teams must work fully from a phone — GPS check-in, photo capture, call logging, lead updates, WhatsApp messaging — in areas with unreliable data coverage. If the app requires a strong internet connection to log a visit, it will fail exactly when the rep is in the field.
5
Real-time field activity dashboards for managers
A field sales manager needs to see — right now — which reps are active, where they have been, how many visits have been completed today and which territories are being neglected. End-of-day reports are too late. Real-time dashboards let managers identify underperformance while there is still time in the day to address it.
How telecrm works for field teams

Verified field activity — not verbal updates.
Real-time dashboards — not end-of-day reports.

telecrm's field team capabilities are built around one principle: every piece of field activity should be captured automatically and verified independently, without relying on rep honesty or memory.

GPS check-ins
Field reps check in at each client location with a GPS-tagged punch. Location coordinates, timestamp and an optional photo are captured and stored against the lead record — creating a verified visit log managers can trust.
Photo capture at visit
Reps upload a photo at each check-in — a storefront, a site, a meeting room — attached to the GPS record. Managers see photographic evidence of the visit alongside location data, giving complete confidence in field activity records.
Territory-based assignment
Assign each field rep a pincode cluster or geographic territory. Leads and accounts within that territory are automatically routed to the right rep — no manual assignment, no coverage gaps, no two reps covering the same area.
Visit history & revisit tracking
Every account's visit history is stored and visible — first visit date, last visit date, total visit count and time since last visit. Managers see which accounts are being visited consistently and which are being neglected within a territory.
Real-time field activity dashboard
Managers see live field activity — which reps are checked in, how many visits each rep has completed today, which territories are active and which have had no activity. Updated in real time as reps check in across the field.
1-click calling from the field
Field reps call leads directly from the telecrm mobile app. Calls are recorded and logged against the lead automatically — so call activity between visits is tracked alongside GPS check-in data in a single lead record.
Geo-based lead routing
When a new lead arrives from any source, telecrm automatically routes it to the field rep whose territory covers that location. No manual routing, no delays, no leads sitting unassigned because no one knew which rep covered that pincode.
Post-visit notes & forms
Reps complete structured visit notes and mandatory forms immediately after each check-in — capturing what was discussed, what was ordered, what objections were raised and what the next step is. Visit data is structured, not just a text note no one reads.
WhatsApp from the lead record
Field reps send WhatsApp messages — order confirmations, follow-up messages, product information — directly from the lead record in the mobile app. Every message is logged automatically, giving managers a complete picture of all client communication across both visits and WhatsApp.
Field teams across industries

Any business with a team
selling face to face on the ground.

Field sales operations vary widely — but the core need is always the same: verified activity data and territory accountability for a team the manager cannot physically supervise.

FMCG & Distribution
Retailer route coverage
FMCG field reps covering retailer routes need GPS check-ins at each outlet, order capture, visit frequency tracking and territory dashboards — so managers know which routes are being covered and which outlets are being missed.
Real Estate
Site visit management
Real estate agents taking buyers to site visits check in at each property with GPS and photo. Inside sales teams see which leads have been taken to site, what was observed and what the buyer's response was — without waiting for the agent to call in.
Automotive
Dealer and channel partner visits
Automotive field sales executives visiting dealerships and channel partners check in at each location, log what was discussed and capture a photo of the visit — giving regional managers verified records of relationship management activity across their territory.
Agriculture & Rural
Farm and village coverage
Agricultural input companies and rural distribution businesses with field officers visiting farms and villages need territory-based assignment and GPS-verified visit logs — especially where mobile connectivity is unreliable and verbal reporting is the current norm.
Quick Commerce
Merchant onboarding field operations
Quick commerce businesses onboarding local merchants through field teams need territory-based rep assignment, GPS check-ins at merchant locations, onboarding form capture and first-order milestone tracking — all in a mobile-first workflow that works on Indian data networks.
Any Industry
Any team that visits customers in person
