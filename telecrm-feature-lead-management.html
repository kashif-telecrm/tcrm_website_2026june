Integrations

Connect your lead sources once and every new enquiry flows in automatically — Facebook, Instagram, WhatsApp, marketplaces, website forms, and more. See all integrations →

Bulk Upload

Got a spreadsheet of leads? Upload via Excel or CSV — map columns to telecrm fields, handle duplicates on the way in, and have hundreds of leads live in minutes.

Manual Creation

Add a lead on the spot — from a walk-in, a referral, or a conversation at an event. The quick-add form captures just the essentials so your rep can move fast.

WhatsApp Message

When someone messages your business WhatsApp for the first time, telecrm automatically creates a lead — complete with the conversation already attached.

Incoming Call

When an unknown number calls in via your connected cloud telephony, telecrm creates a new lead instantly — no manual entry required from your rep.

Lead De-duplication

One person. One record.
No confusion.

Duplicate leads are inevitable when leads come from multiple sources. telecrm gives you the tools to catch them early, merge them cleanly, and resolve conflicts without losing any data.

Manual lead merge

When telecrm flags a duplicate, your rep can merge both records into one — choosing which values to keep for each field. All history from both records is preserved.

See how conflict resolution works →

Deduplication during bulk upload

When uploading leads via Excel, telecrm runs two deduplication checks simultaneously:

Within the Excel sheet itself — catches repeated entries before they enter telecrm
Against existing records in telecrm — flags leads that already exist and lets you decide whether to update, skip, or merge

360° Lead View

Everything about a lead.
One screen. Zero switching.

Open any lead and see their complete history — every call, every WhatsApp, every task, every note. Your rep always knows exactly where they left off. No context lost. No tools to switch between.

Every field value, every status update, every stage change — with timestamp and the rep who made it.

Assignment History
