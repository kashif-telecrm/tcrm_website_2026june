Problems Hub · Lead Follow-Up

Most deals don't die on the first call.
They die on the second one that never happens.

Reminders are a hassle to set, so reps skip them. Missed follow-ups don't surface anywhere. Every rep follows up on their own clock — and deals close for competitors who simply showed up again.

Where it breaks down

Six problems your team
is probably living with right now.

Problem 01
Setting reminders takes more time than the call
Open the lead, open the calendar, type the time, paste the number, switch back. By the time a reminder is set, the next lead is already cooling. Reps skip the reminder because the friction isn't worth it.
Problem 02
Missed follow-ups don't surface anywhere
A lead is supposed to be called back on Tuesday. Tuesday becomes Thursday. Thursday becomes never. There's no SLA timer, no overdue list, no daily 'leads you forgot' view. The miss stays invisible.
Problem 03
Every rep follows up on their own clock
No team cadence, no minimum touchpoints, no rules per stage. One rep follows up five times in a week. Another calls once and gives up. The variance in follow-up behaviour directly explains the variance in close rates.
Problem 04
Follow-ups dead-end on one channel
The rep calls. Phone goes unanswered. That's it — the follow-up dies on a ringtone. No automated WhatsApp ping, no email backup, no second attempt scheduled. One unanswered call writes off the lead.
Problem 05
Birthdays, EMIs, renewals — all forgotten
The most natural reasons to call back — a buyer's birthday, an EMI date, a SIP rollover, an insurance renewal — get buried in spreadsheets nobody opens. The call that should be effortless never happens.
Problem 06
Managers can't see who's actually following up
The leaderboard rewards call volume, not follow-up adherence. Reps cherry-pick easy fresh leads and skip the harder follow-ups that need real work. Managers find out at the month-end review, not in time to act.
How telecrm fixes it

Six fixes — one platform.

Every capability below is available on mobile and desktop, configured for your team in hours, and running the moment you go live.

1-click follow-up scheduling
Set a follow-up directly from the lead card. Tap a preset — 2 hours, tomorrow, next Monday — or pick a custom time. Done. No calendar switching, no manual note-taking, no friction. The reminder is set before the call ends.
Daily follow-up view for reps
Every rep opens their day to one view — today's follow-ups, overdue follow-ups, leads cooling past SLA. Nothing falls off the radar. Managers see the same view across the whole team — who is on track, who is slipping.
Stage-level cadence rules
Define what a Fresh, Warm or Hot lead should look like — call within X hours, message within Y days, retry up to Z times before escalation. The CRM enforces the cadence. Reps don't decide; the system does.
Multi-channel automated follow-up
Call goes unanswered? An automated WhatsApp goes out within minutes. Two calls missed? An email kicks in. The follow-up shifts to whichever channel the lead is most likely to respond on — without the rep lifting a finger.
Custom date triggers for renewals
Set follow-up triggers on custom date fields — policy renewal, EMI date, loan maturity, course end. The CRM fires the right action at the right time automatically. The most valuable calls happen without anyone remembering to make them.
Follow-up adherence dashboard
See every overdue follow-up, every skipped cadence and every lead cooling past SLA — across the whole team, by rep, by stage. Managers spot the slippage early. Coaching is specific, not general. Deals that were going cold get saved.
Also worth knowing

Built for the way
Indian sales teams actually work.

Works for any follow-up cadence
B2B long cycles, high-volume inside sales, BFSI renewals, real estate drip — the cadence rules adapt to your sales model. One platform handles the 3-touch fast close and the 90-day nurture sequence equally well.
Reps get reminders, not chaos
Reminders surface on the rep's phone at the right time — a push notification, a lead card alert, a position at the top of their daily list. The right call at the right moment, without reps checking a separate calendar.
Escalation when leads go cold
When a lead exceeds its SLA without a follow-up, telecrm alerts the manager and can escalate to a senior rep automatically. Cold leads are surfaced before they're lost — not discovered in an audit three months later.
Drip sequences that run themselves
Build multi-step follow-up sequences — call on day 1, WhatsApp on day 3, email on day 7, call again on day 10. Sequences run automatically once a lead enters the right stage. Consistent outreach without any manual coordination.
