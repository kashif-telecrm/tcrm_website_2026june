SIM-Based Calling vs Cloud Telephony

SIM-Based Calling beats Cloud Telephony
for most Indian sales teams.

Cloud telephony sounds useful in theory. But for field sales reps, relationship managers, and anyone who sells on the move — it introduces friction, cost, and lower answer rates. SIM-based calling through telecrm keeps your reps calling the way they already do, while giving you the tracking and visibility you need.

SIM-Based Calling
Recommended

Leads recognise and trust a real mobile number. Unknown business numbers get ignored — real numbers get answered.

Mobile networks are inherently more stable than internet-based calls. No lag, no drops, no robotic audio on a bad WiFi day.

Reps call from their own phone the way they always have. No new apps, no new numbers, no friction to adoption.

A call from a real number feels human. Leads are more likely to engage, open up, and trust your rep from the first call.

Calls run on the rep's existing SIM recharge. No monthly platform fees, no per-minute billing surprises.

Cloud Telephony
For desk-based teams

Business numbers are unfamiliar. Leads see an unknown number and let it ring out — your rep never gets the conversation started.

Call clarity depends entirely on internet connectivity. A weak connection means a broken conversation and a lost impression.

High-volume cloud numbers are routinely marked as spam by carriers, killing pickup rates overnight with no warning.

Monthly platform fees, per-minute billing, third-party dialer apps, and integration overhead add up fast and add friction.

Switching reps to a new dialer app creates resistance, workarounds, and gaps in your call data that undermine your reporting.

When does cloud telephony make sense?

Cloud telephony has its place — particularly for large enterprises with dedicated call centres, complex inbound routing needs, or teams making international calls at scale. If that's you, telecrm integrates with leading cloud telephony providers including CallerDesk, Maqsam, MCube, and Knowlarity.

01
Large call centres with inbound routing
If your team handles thousands of inbound calls daily with complex IVR menus, queue management, and multi-tier routing — a cloud telephony system is the right infrastructure. telecrm integrates with it seamlessly.
02
International calling at scale
For teams calling across multiple countries where local SIM coverage is inconsistent, VoIP-based cloud calling offers cost advantages and centralised number management that SIM-based calling cannot match.
03
Desk-bound inside sales teams
If your reps are always at a desk, internet connectivity is reliable, and calls are made from a shared pool of business numbers — cloud telephony works well. telecrm tracks every call regardless of which system routes it.

telecrm works with both — SIM-based calling and cloud telephony.

IVR Integration

Calling List Management

Build targeted calling lists from your lead database using filters — by stage, source, geography, assigned rep, or any custom field. Give each rep a focused list to work through daily. Replace scattered spreadsheets with structured, trackable calling campaigns that move at scale.

Predictive Dialing

telecrm automatically queues and dials the next lead the moment a call ends. Reps spend zero time deciding who to call next — the system keeps them moving continuously. More calls per hour, more conversations per day, more deals closed per week.

Sticky Agent

When an existing lead calls in, telecrm automatically routes the call to the sales rep that lead is assigned to. No re-introductions, no lost context, no broken relationships. The lead always reaches their person — building trust and improving the chance of conversion.
