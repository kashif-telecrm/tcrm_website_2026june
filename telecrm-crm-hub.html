Why two approaches?

A CRM is only useful if it's
configured for how you work.

Most CRM software is built for a generic business. You get one structure, one workflow, one way of seeing your data — and you're expected to adapt your team around it. The result is low adoption, incomplete data, and managers flying blind.

The problem

When a CRM doesn't match how your team sells, reps work around it instead of with it. Calls go unlogged. Leads fall through. Managers request reports that no one has time to compile. The CRM becomes a cost instead of an asset.

This is not a training problem. It is a fit problem.

The telecrm answer

telecrm is built to fit around you — not the other way around. Instead of forcing you into one mould, we offer two entry points: how your business sells, and how your team is structured.

Either path leads to the same platform — configured exactly for your reality.

Understanding the two approaches

Two different questions.
Both lead to the right answer.

Approach 1
CRM by Type

Every business has a primary channel — some close deals over the phone, others through WhatsApp, others through high-volume inbound enquiries, others through field visits. The channel determines everything: what your reps need in front of them, what your manager needs to see, and what automations save the most time.

If you know your sales motion — outbound, inbound, digital, field, relationship-driven — this is the faster path to the right configuration.

Approach 2
CRM by Team

This approach starts with a different question: how is your team organised?

The structure of your team determines what the CRM needs to do. A call centre needs agent queues, recording, and quality audits. A field team needs mobile-first access and visit logging. An enterprise team needs role-based access, multi-team pipelines, and regional reporting. A small team needs simplicity and fast setup.

If you can describe how your team is structured more easily than how they sell, this is the right entry point.
