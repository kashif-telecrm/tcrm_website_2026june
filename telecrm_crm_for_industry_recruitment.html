The recruitment agency challenge

Recruitment agencies manage two pipelines simultaneously —
and most only manage one properly.

A recruitment agency is running two parallel markets at the same time. On the candidate side: sourcing, screening, shortlisting, interview scheduling, offer follow-up and post-joining check-ins. On the client side: mandate intake, requirement clarification, candidate submission, interview coordination, offer negotiation and invoice follow-up. Both pipelines are active simultaneously, both have multiple stages, and both involve time-sensitive follow-up that cannot slip.

Most recruitment agencies manage candidates well — because placing candidates is how they earn. Client relationship management gets neglected because it feels less urgent, until a key client moves their mandates to a competitor who stayed in better contact. telecrm gives both pipelines equal structure, equal visibility and equal follow-up discipline.

What telecrm solves for recruitment
telecrm gives recruitment agencies separate structured pipelines for candidates and clients — with automated follow-up sequences for both sides, placement milestone tracking, WhatsApp Chat Sync for complete communication history and team performance dashboards that show exactly where every active search stands.
What recruitment agencies need from a CRM

Five things that keep both sides of the market
moving at the same time.

Recruitment CRM requirements are uniquely two-sided. A standard sales CRM manages one pipeline — prospects becoming customers. A recruitment CRM needs to manage candidates becoming placements and clients becoming repeat mandates, simultaneously, with different follow-up rules for each.

1
Separate pipelines for candidates and clients
A candidate pipeline tracks individuals through sourced, screened, shortlisted, submitted, interview scheduled, interview completed, offer made, offer accepted, joined, post-joining check-in. A client pipeline tracks organisations through mandate received, requirements clarified, candidates submitted, interview feedback received, offer stage, placement confirmed, invoice raised, payment received. These are fundamentally different pipelines with different stages, different owners and different follow-up rules — they cannot share a single pipeline without losing clarity on both.
2
Time-sensitive follow-up on both sides
A candidate who has not heard back after an interview loses interest and accepts another offer. A client who submitted a mandate two weeks ago and received no update assumes the agency is not working on it and calls a competitor. Both situations are avoidable with timely, automated follow-up — a candidate status update WhatsApp two days after an interview, a client update call once a week while a search is active. Without automation, recruiters miss these touchpoints when managing multiple active searches simultaneously.
3
Complete communication history for every candidate and client
A candidate who was screened six months ago and rejected for one role may be perfect for a new mandate. When a recruiter calls that candidate again, they need to know what was discussed previously — which roles they were considered for, what their salary expectations were, why they were rejected, what their current situation is. Without a complete interaction history, every call starts from zero and the candidate experience suffers.
4
Post-placement follow-up for replacement guarantees and repeat mandates
Most recruitment agencies offer a replacement guarantee if a placed candidate leaves within a defined period. Without a system tracking every placement's joining date and the guarantee window, agencies miss their obligation to proactively check in — and find out about a candidate leaving from an angry client rather than a scheduled follow-up. The same post-placement relationship, maintained well, is also the most reliable source of the next mandate from the same client.
5
Recruiter accountability and search status visibility
A recruitment manager overseeing a team of recruiters each handling multiple active searches needs to know — without asking individually — how many candidates each recruiter has in each stage, which client mandates have had no candidate submissions in the past week, which placements are approaching their guarantee window. Without a system providing this visibility, management is reactive and search quality varies unpredictably across the team.
How telecrm works for recruitment

Both pipelines. Both markets.
One system managing all of it.

telecrm is used by staffing agencies, executive search firms, recruitment process outsourcing businesses and internal talent acquisition teams across India. Futwork is among the recruitment businesses that have used telecrm to manage high-volume staffing operations.

Candidate pipeline
Track every candidate through every stage — sourced, screened, shortlisted, submitted, interview scheduled, offer made, joined, post-joining check-in. Each stage has defined follow-up rules and automated reminders so no candidate waits too long for an update and no stage is skipped under pressure.
Client mandate pipeline
Track every client mandate from intake through requirements clarification, candidate submission, interview coordination, offer stage and placement confirmation. Client updates are automated weekly while a search is active — so clients know their mandate is being worked without the recruiter manually drafting every update.
1-click calling with auto-logging
Recruiters call candidates and clients directly from the record. Every call is recorded and logged automatically — so the full call history is part of the candidate or client record, visible to the whole team. When a candidate calls back, whoever picks up has the complete conversation history.
WhatsApp Chat Sync
All candidate and client WhatsApp conversations are synced automatically to the record via WhatsApp Chat Sync. When a recruiter leaves, every conversation they had with every candidate and client stays with the agency — so the team can pick up any relationship without starting from zero.
Automated follow-up sequences
Automated WhatsApp sequences trigger at every key stage — post-interview status updates to candidates, weekly mandate updates to clients, offer follow-up reminders, joining date confirmations and post-joining check-ins. Recruiters focus on relationship-building rather than remembering who to call when.
Post-placement milestone tracking
Every placed candidate has a joining date and guarantee window tracked in telecrm. Automated check-in sequences trigger at 30 days, 60 days and the end of the guarantee period — so the agency proactively manages every placement rather than discovering problems when a client complains.
Recruiter performance dashboards
Managers see each recruiter's active candidate count, submissions per mandate, interview-to-offer conversion, placement volume and invoice pipeline — in real time. Which recruiters are most productive, which searches have stalled and which clients are overdue for a check-in — all visible without a team meeting.
Calling campaigns for bulk sourcing
When a new mandate requires reaching out to a large pool of candidates quickly, telecrm's calling campaign feature assigns batches of candidates to recruiters with structured follow-up sequences — so sourcing for a new requirement is a managed process rather than a team huddle around a spreadsheet.
Client repeat mandate pipeline
After a successful placement, the client relationship moves into an account management pipeline — scheduled check-ins, satisfaction follow-up and proactive outreach when the client's next hiring window approaches. Repeat mandates from existing clients are the most profitable and fastest-closing business a recruitment agency can win.
Recruitment business types

telecrm works for every type of
recruitment and staffing business.

The recruitment model differs between a volume staffing agency, an executive search firm and an RPO provider — but the core need is the same: both pipelines managed, both markets followed up, every placement tracked.

Staffing Agencies
High-volume candidate management
Staffing agencies placing large volumes of candidates across multiple clients simultaneously need bulk sourcing campaigns, automated candidate follow-up sequences and real-time dashboards showing each recruiter's pipeline — so high volume does not mean high dropout from poor follow-up.
Executive Search
Long-cycle senior search management
Executive search firms managing 3 to 6 month searches for senior roles need a CRM that tracks the full candidate engagement lifecycle — multiple conversations, reference checks, offer negotiation stages — and the client communication cadence that keeps a senior mandate engaged over a long search timeline.
RPO Providers
Multi-client talent acquisition
RPO businesses managing recruitment for multiple client organisations simultaneously need separate candidate pools per client, client-specific pipeline stages and role-based access that prevents one client's candidate data from being visible to another client's account team.
Contract Staffing
Contract renewal and extension tracking
Contract staffing businesses managing contractors on rolling assignments need contract end date tracking, automated renewal outreach to clients 60 days before expiry and contractor check-ins that surface any dissatisfaction before it results in an unexpected exit.
Campus Recruitment
Bulk hiring and placement drives
Organisations and agencies running campus hiring drives need bulk calling campaigns to engage large candidate pools, structured shortlisting pipelines and offer follow-up sequences that maintain candidate engagement between selection and joining — when dropout rates are highest.
Freelance Marketplaces
Gig worker onboarding at scale
Platforms onboarding gig workers and freelancers at scale need bulk candidate outreach, structured onboarding pipelines and automated milestone communications — from application received through document verification to first assignment confirmation — managed systematically across thousands of simultaneous onboarding journeys.
